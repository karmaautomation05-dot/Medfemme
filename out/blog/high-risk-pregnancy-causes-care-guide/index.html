High-Risk Pregnancy – Causes, Risks, and How to Ensure a Healthy Pregnancy

Dr. Priyanka Bhargava
Expert Obstetrics & Gynecology Care

Pregnancy is a beautiful journey, but some pregnancies require additional medical attention and monitoring. These are known as high-risk pregnancies.

What is a High-Risk Pregnancy?

A pregnancy may be considered high-risk if the mother has pre-existing medical conditions such as:

  • Diabetes
  • Thyroid disorders
  • High blood pressure
  • Heart disease

Risk Factors

  • Age: Women younger than 18 or older than 35.
  • Multiple Pregnancies: Twins or triplets.
  • Previous Complications: History of miscarriages or preterm birth.
  • Lifestyle: Obesity, smoking, or poor nutrition.

Common Warning Signs

Contact your doctor immediately if you experience:

  • Vaginal bleeding
  • Severe abdominal pain
  • Blurred vision
  • Severe swelling
  • Persistent headaches
  • Reduced fetal movements

Managing a High-Risk Pregnancy

  • Regular antenatal check-ups.
  • Following a healthy, doctor-recommended diet.
  • Timely screening tests and continuous monitoring.

Frequently Asked Questions

What makes a pregnancy high-risk?

Factors like age (under 18 or over 35), pre-existing conditions (diabetes, BP), or multiple babies (twins).

Can a high-risk pregnancy be normal?

Yes, with proper monitoring and medical guidance, most high-risk pregnancies result in healthy babies.

How often do I need check-ups?

High-risk pregnancies usually require more frequent visits and specialized scans.

What are the warning signs?

Bleeding, severe abdominal pain, blurred vision, or reduced fetal movement.
